## Self-heal log (process learnings)

### 1. The "+$2.54 INJ trade" attribution dance
Initially identified the trade as sentinel-trader. Couldn't find the cloid in core's database. Walked back the identification. User pushed back: "we absolutely knew who set it." I had been right — sentinel-trader writes to its OWN SQLite. Lesson: **trust the identification, search in the right database.**

### 2. Sentinel council audited the wrong thing
Ran a deep audit on my session decisions. Council returned CRITICAL on enabling sentinel-trader live, citing the -$9.51 7-day net as risk. Operator overrode: "the -$9.51 was from a bug — fucks me off that the profitable engines were pulled because the bug had been killed anyway." **Lesson: every sentinel audit needs the operator's ground-truth on root causes, not just observable metrics.** → Created `FIX_TIMELINE.md` policy as institutional memory.

### 3. The 56.3% WR fossil
Reported hl_settle_5m as "WR 56.3% / PF 1.38" — operator caught it: "56% hl settle is not post fix - wr is higher pf is higher." Segmented closures by fix timestamp: last 10 trades WR 100% PF ∞, last 30 PF 3.70. **Lesson hit twice in one session.** The fix wasn't memorized — only logged.

### 4. "Sentinel-trader is not an engine"
Dashboard labeled all sentinel-trader positions as engine = "sentinel-trader". Operator: "What engine is sentinel trader? I need the engine attribution not the project manager." Updated to show actual generators (donchian, ema_cross, rsi_revert, bb_squeeze). **Lesson: the harness is not the engine.**

### 5. Active vs firing generators
Even after fix #4, label was misleading — showed all NON-MUTED generators ("sw:donchian+rsi_revert") implying confluence when actually only donchian voted. Patched to read `gen_ids` from signals table (firing-truth) instead of `disabled_gens` inversion (allowed-to-vote). **Lesson: the dashboard should show what fired, not what could have.**

### 6. Sizing instinct vs system design
After seeing 6 positions all donchian, asked "should we move stops to BE?" Pushed back firmly with three reasons (inconsistent with UZT_REV ethos / breaks backtested edge / statistical cost). Operator agreed: "Go." **Lesson: trail-stops are the engine's risk management. BE moves replace calibrated mechanism with heuristic.**
